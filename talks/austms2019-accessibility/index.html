Hi! I'm hard of hearing!

P.S. 'Hearing impaired' is considered politically incorrect.

Hearing aid on the left. Deaf on the right.

In a conference, when you walk into a room/lecture theatre, what do you think about?

Where's the best place for me to sit?

  • I want to sit somewhere towards the right since I can only hear out of my left.
  • Is there an audio loop available?
  • Will the speaker be standing or talking? Will I be able to see their lips?
  • Will the speaker be using a microphone? If not, I'm going to need to sit closer.

How can we do things such that people with disabilities don't have to think about all that?

Accessibility (#a11y):
Performing actions and designing products/services/ environments so that barriers are minimised for people with disabilities.

Speaking Tips

  • Speak clearly; avoid mumbling; enunciate every syllable.
  • Ensure your face/mouth is visible to the audience.
  • Avoid filler words (e.g. 'you know', 'anyway') and excessive exposition.
  • Repetition is OK.
  • More important for larger rooms: use the mic (regardless of how well you think you can project your voice)
  • Repeat/paraphrase audience questions being asked (especially if a mic is not given to audience members).

Content Tips

  • Captions/transcriptions for video/audio content.
    • $$$: Professional captioning/transcription services exist.
    • DIY: Youtube & WebCaptioner generates captions that you can download and edit.
  • Ensure content is available in multiple forms (e.g. lecture recordings + sufficient notes).
    Moving to a blended learning model is one good way to ensure this.
  • Make lecture content available to students before the lecture.

Takeaway Advice for Maths on the Web:

Avoid using images of mathematical equations. Write in TeX/LaTeX and convert to MathJax or (ideally) MathML.
