Hi! I'm hard of hearing!

P.S. 'Hearing impaired' is considered politically incorrect.

Hearing aid on the left. Deaf on the right.

In a conference, when you walk into a room/lecture theatre, what do you think about?

Where's the best place for me to sit?

  • I want to sit somewhere towards the right since I can only hear out of my left.
  • Is there an audio loop available?
  • Will the speaker be standing or talking? Will I be able to see their lips?
  • Will the speaker be using a microphone? If not, I'm going to need to sit closer.

How can we design spaces/resources/events such that people with disabilities don't have to think about all these extra considerations?

Auditory

Hearing Loss

Not all sound is perceived.

Auditory Processing Disorders

Sound perceived normally, but brain has processing issues.

Speaking Tips

  • Use the mic (regardless of how well you think you can project your voice)
  • Repetition is encouraged.
  • Repeat/paraphrase audience questions being asked.
  • Speak clearly; avoid mumbling; enunciate every syllable.

Content Tips

  • Captions/transcriptions for video/audio content.
    • $$$: Professional captioning/transcription services exist.
    • DIY: Youtube & WebCaptioner generates captions that you can download and edit.
  • Ensure content is available in both written & video form.
    Moving to a blended learning model is one good way to ensure this.

An ideal:

Live Streaming;
Online Conferences & Webinars

Benefits:

  • Allows individuals control over their own environment.
  • Automated captioning can be integrated.
  • Lower overall costs (flights/accommodation, organising/catering).
  • Enables students & ECRs to get in on the action!

Potential Hurdles:

  • Infrastructure/organisation for large conferences/events.
  • Adoption/awareness of appropriate software/technology.

Advice: Present/observe a small-scale webinar
(e.g. Global Math Project, AustMS/AMSI Teaching Seminar Series) or online class.
